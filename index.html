Finding words in speech

In this project we investigated how Brazilian adults deal with multiple statistics to discover words in fluent speech. We created speech streams with transitional and phonotactic probabilities that work together, helping segmentation, or collide, hindering segmentation. Turns out that the combination of these statistics is really important for segmentation. The article is available on PsyArXiv and is published on Memory & Cognition.

Bilingualism & infant cognition

In this project we investigated the famous "cognitive advantage" that bilingual infants are said to posses. We ran two studies of our own (with 7 and 20-months-old) and we also re-analyzed three open datasets. Overall, we found a tradeoff between speed of learning and flexibility to update learning. Our findings points to adaptations rather than advantages for both mono and bilinguals. The preprint is available at the PsyArXiv and the article is under review.

ManyBabies

Pupillometry

Pupillometry is awesome, but hard. When running pupillometry experiments it is necessary to control for the luminance of visual stimuli. In this project, I've adapted the SHINE MATLAB toolbox to control for several low-level properties of colorful images (SHINE_color)—which are much more attractive to infants than black and white stimuli. Obs. sample image from NOUN database.

Cross-situational word learning

In this project we are conducting a meta-analysis and systematic review of the empirical studies using the cross-situational word learning design. From 2007 up to 2019 we found 55 studies with hundreds of participants (from infants to older adults). Part of the meta-analysis, with infants/children, is openly available at Metalab (domain: "early language"; dataset: "cross-situational word learning"). We are now updating the review and running analysis on the adult data.

Learning & Automatic Reinforcement

In this project we investigated whether explicit versus automatic reinforcement would have a greater effect on 4-years-old children's descriptions. I presented a picture to the participant and described it in passive voice, then I showed the participant a similar picture and asked she to describe it. If she described in passive voice, nothing happened, but if she described in active voice, explicit reinforcement, with favorite videos and compliments followed. What happened? Check the article at The Analysis of Verbal Behavior. :-)

Statistical language learning

In this book chapter, we introduce recent research in statistical language learning. We cover four big topics: 1) Discovering the sounds in language, 2) Finding words in speech, 3) Mapping words to meaning, and 4) Learning rudimentary grammar. It offers a great and gentler introduction for undergraduate and graduate students.

Critical periods

In this lecture, I cover critical periods on language development, from the womb to the first anniversary. Some of the topics include how babies' perceptual attunement, speech segmentation, cross-situational word learning, and phonotactics. This lecture have been well received by undergraduate and graduate students from Brazil and Canada. (Image: Werker & Hensch, 2015)

Basic learning

In this semester-long course, I cover the principles of respondent (reflex) and operant learning. I present the variation & selection framework and discuss the concept of behavioral repertoire. I also cover some learning procedures such as stimulus pairing, shaping, reinforcement, punishment, extinction, and stimulus equivalence.

Experimental analysis of behavior

In this laboratory course, I introduce students to the selection by consequences framework in a practical way. We do a series of learning exercises with non-human subjects (lab rats) using the Skinner box. Some procedures that I cover are: simple and conditional discrimination, shaping, reinforcement, extinction, fading, and behavioral chaining, etc. (Image: Skinner, 1957)
